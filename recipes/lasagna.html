Lasagna Recipe

Classic Lasagna with Bolognese Sauce

Lasagna alla Bolognese is a refined Italian masterpiece from the Emilia-Romagna region, celebrated for its "elegant simplicity" and melt-in-the-mouth texture. Unlike American-style versions that rely on heavy ricotta, this authentic dish is defined by its delicate layers of thin pasta, slow-cooked meat ragù, and silky béchamel sauce.

Ingredients

  1. Ragu alla Bolognese (Meat Sauce)

    2. A traditional ragù focuses on a deep, savory meat flavor rather than a heavy tomato base.

    • The Meats: 1 lb ground beef and 1 lb ground pork (or a mix of beef, pork, and veal). Finely diced pancetta (about 2–4 oz) is often added for extra depth.
    • Aromatics (Fragrant Vegetables): 1 medium onion, 1–2 carrots, and 1–2 celery stalks, all very finely minced.
    • Liquids: 1 cup dry red or white wine (for deglazing) and 1–2 cups whole milk (to tenderize the meat).
    • Tomatoes: 1 can (28 oz) of crushed tomatoes or whole peeled tomatoes (crushed by hand) and 2–3 tbsp tomato paste.
    • Seasonings: Fresh bay leaves, salt, and freshly cracked black pepper. Some chefs add a pinch of clove or cinnamon.
  2. Bechamel Sauce(Besciamella)

    3. This creamy white sauce replaces the ricotta found in American-style lasagna.

    • Roux Base: 4–5 tbsp unsalted butter and 1/4 to 1/3 cup all-purpose flour.
    • Liquid: 3–4 cups whole milk, preferably warmed before adding to the roux.
    • Flavoring: A generous pinch of freshly grated nutmeg, salt, and white or black pepper.
  3. Pasta and Cheese
    • Lasagna Sheets: 1 lb of fresh egg pasta sheets (plain or spinach) or 12–15 high-quality "no-boil" or dried lasagna noodles.
    • Parmigiano-Reggiano: While not strictly traditional for Bolognese, many 2026 recipes include 1 lb of fresh mozzarella (diced or sliced) for additional meltiness.
    • Mozzarella (Optional): While not strictly traditional for Bolognese, many 2026 recipes include 1 lb of fresh mozzarella (diced or sliced) for additional meltiness.
